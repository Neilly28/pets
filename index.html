Why Pets In Peace Aquamation?

Gentler

Aquamation is cremation using water instead of fire. Through a process called Alkaline Hydrolysis, it uses a combination of flowing water, temperature, and alkalinity to accelerate the natural course of tissue and chemical breakdown.

Greener

Aquamation is environmentally friendly. It has a significantly lower carbon footprint than traditional fire cremation. It does not produce mercury emissions nor other toxic air emissions. The water at the end of the process is safe to handle, safe for the sewer system and even makes an excellent fertilizer for plants.

Gives Back

Our founding team is composed of animal welfare advocates who are active in animal rescue. Part of our proceeds is dedicated to supporting animal rescue efforts, stray feeding and spay & neuter programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Aquamation?

Aquamation is water cremation through a process called Alkaline Hydrolysis. It uses a combination of flowing water, temperature, and alkalinity to accelerate the natural course of tissue and chemical breakdown. At the end of this process, the only solid remains are the bones, which are ground and returned to the family as 'ashes'. Our water cremation machine is officially sourced from Aquamation International in Australia, the industry leader in Alkaline Hydrolysis technology.
